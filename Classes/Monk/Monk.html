Whatever their discipline, monks are united in their ability to harness the energy that flows in their bodies. Whether channeled as a striking display of combat prowess or a subtler focus of defensive ability and speed, this energy infuses all that a monk does.

Some monks choose to direct their energy into their bodies, shifting their fists into natural weapons or enhancing their speed and movement beyond normal bounds. Others hone their minds, creating magical effects through meditation. Yet all monks, whatever their focus, are united in an absolute control of their bodies that allows them to move and fight with exceptional speed and skill.

The Energy of Life

Monks make careful study of the natural energy of life. This energy is an element of the magic that suffuses the universe—specifically, the element that flows through living bodies. Monks harness this power within themselves to create magical effects and exceed their bodies' physical capabilities, and some of their special attacks can hinder the flow of energy in their opponents. Using this energy, monks channel uncanny speed and strength into their unarmed strikes. As they gain experience, their martial training and their mastery of vital energy gives them more power over their bodies and the bodies of their foes.

Perfect Discipline

Monks are set apart from the common warrior by their abilities, which ride the border between natural and magical. While most soldiers and fighters rely on weapons and armor to win battles, monks rely primarily on their bodies and skills to see them through. Monks use the perfect control they maintain over their bodies to move like no other, dodging blows and harm with ease and delivering precise, powerful strikes.

Some monks live in community with the surrounding population, honing their discipline against the distractions of the world. Others are sworn to isolation, emerging only to serve as spies or assassins at the command of their leader, a noble patron, or some other mortal or divine power.

For most monks, becoming an adventurer means leaving a structured lifestyle to become a wanderer. This can be a harsh transition, and monks don't undertake it lightly. Those who leave their homes take their work seriously, approaching their adventures as personal tests of their physical and spiritual growth. As a rule, monks are driven by a desire to accomplish a greater mission than merely slaying monsters and plundering their treasure.

Creating a Monk

As you make your monk character, think about how you learned your skills and spent your formative years. Were you an orphan or a child left on a monastery's threshold? Did your parents promise you to an old master when your skills became apparent? Did you enter this regimented life to hide from a crime you committed? Or did you choose the monastic life for yourself?

Consider why you began adventuring. Were you chosen for a particularly important mission? Perhaps you were cast out of your community because of some rule violation. Did you dread leaving, or were you happy to go? Is there something you hope to accomplish in your adventures? Are you eager to return to your home?

You must have an Dexterity score of 13 or higher in order to multiclass into this class.

Class Features

As a monk, you gain the following class features.

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d8 per monk level

Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier

Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d8 + your Constitution modifier per monk level after 1st.

Proficiencies

Armor: Light armor

Weapons: Simple weapons, martial weapons with the light property, improvised weapons

Tools: Choose one type of artisan's tools or one musical instrument

Saving Throws: Strength, Dexterity

Skills: Choose two from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Religion and Stealth

Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) a shortsword or (b) any simple weapon
  • (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
  • 10 darts
Alternatively, you can buy your own equipment, starting with 31 gp, 3 sp, and 4 cp.

Unarmored Defense

Beginning at 1st level, you gain one of the following benefits of your choice. At 9th level, you gain an additional benefit.

  • Feats of Might: While you are wearing no armor and not wielding a shield, you can add your Strength modifier to your Armor Class.
  • Defense of the Intellect: While you are wearing no armor and not wielding a shield, you can add your Intelligence modifier to your Armor Class.
  • Combat Instincts: While you are wearing no armor and not wielding a shield, you can add your Wisdom modifier to your Armor Class.
  • Dance of Battle: While you are wearing no armor and not wielding a shield, you can add your Charisma modifier to your Armor Class.

When you gain a level in this class, you can replace one of your chosen benefits with another benefit of your choice.

Dexterous Combat

At 1st level, you have learned to fight nimbly and with precision. You can use Dexterity instead of Strength for the attack and damage rolls of your unarmed strikes and monk weapons, which are simple melee weapons, improvised weapons, and any martial melee weapons that have the light property. In addition, when you would make an Athletics check in an attempt to grapple or shove a creature, you can instead make an Acrobatics check.

Also, while you are unarmed or wielding only monk weapons, and you aren't wielding a shield, you can use a bonus action on your to make one unarmed strike.

Martial Arts

At 1st level, your practice of martial arts gives you mastery of combat styles that use unarmed strikes and monk weapons. Your unarmed strikes are considered light weapons, and you can roll a d6 in place of the normal damage of your unarmed strike and in place of the normal damage die of a monk weapon. This die becomes a d8 at 6th level, a d10 at 11th level, and a d12 at 17th level.

Unarmored Movement

At 2nd level, while you are not wearing medium or heavy armor or wielding a shield, your speed increases by 10 feet and you can use your Dexterity score instead of your Strength score to calculate your jump distance. This bonus increases when you reach certain monk levels, as shown in the Monk table.

At 9th level, you gain the ability to move along vertical surfaces and across liquids on your turn without falling during the move, you ignore difficult terrain, and your jump distance is doubled.

Energy Points

Also starting at 2nd level, your training allows you to harness the energy of life within you. This energy is represented by a number of energy points. Your monk level determines the number of points you have, as shown in the energy points column of the Monk table. You can spend energy points to fuel various effects, which you gain as you level up.

When you spend an energy point, it is unavailable until you finish a short or long rest, at the end of which you draw all of your expended energy back into yourself.

Some of your energy point features require your target to make a saving throw to resist the feature's effects. The saving throw DC is calculated as follows:

Energy save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Dexterity modifier

Flurry of Blows

Immediately after you take the Attack action or spend an energy point as part of an action on your turn, you can spend 1 energy point to make two unarmed strikes as a bonus action.

At 10th level, the number of unarmed strikes made as part of this bonus action increases to three.

Deflect Attacks

Starting at 2nd level, when you are hit by a weapon attack, you can use your reaction to deflect or catch the attack. When you do so, the damage you take from the attack is reduced by 1d10 + your Dexterity modifier + your monk level.

If you reduce a ranged weapon attack's damage to 0, you can immediately spend 1 energy point as part of the same reaction to make a ranged attack against the creature that attacked you. You make this attack with proficiency, regardless of your weapon proficiencies, and the attack deals an amount of damage equal to two rolls of your Martial Arts die plus your Dexterity modifier.

If you reduce a melee weapon attack's damage to 0, you can immediately spend 1 energy point as part of the same reaction to redirect the force and make an unarmed strike against a creature you can see within 5 feet of yourself. If the attack hits, roll your Martial Arts die twice instead of once and add the result together when determining damage.

Monastic Tradition

At 3rd level, you commit yourself to a monastric tradition. Choose to follow one of the ways of the Ascendant Dragon, the Astral Self, the Caffeinated Master, the Four Elements, the Kensei, the Living Weapon, the Long Death, the Mercy, the Open Hand, the Shadow, or the Sun Soul, Your choice grants you features at 3rd level and again at 6th, 11th, and 17th level.

Slow Fall

Beginning at 3rd level, you can use your reaction when you fall to reduce any falling damage you take by an amount equal to five times your monk level.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, increase two ability scores of your choice by 1, or gain one feat of your choice. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Extra Attack

Beginning at 5th level, you can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

Stunning Strike

Also at 5th level, you can interfere with the flow of energy in an opponent's body. Once per turn, when you hit another creature with a monk weapon or an unarmed strike, you can spend 1 energy point to attempt a stunning strike. The target must make a Constitution saving throw. On a failure, it is stunned until the start of your next turn. On a success, its speed is halved until the start of your next turn, and the next attack against it has advantage.

Energy Strikes

Starting at 6th level, your training empowers your strikes to deal great damage. Your attacks deal double damage to objects and structures, and your unarmed strikes count as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.

Evasion

At 7th level, your instinctive agility lets you dodge out of the way of certain area effects, such as a blue dragon's lightning breath or a fireball spell. When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, and only half damage if you fail.

Iron Will

Also at 7th level, at the end of each of your turns, you can end one effect on yourself causing you to be charmed or frightened.

Perfect Deflection

Beginning at 9th level, when you or a creature within 5 feet of you is hit by a ranged weapon attack, you can use your reaction to spend 3 energy points. If you do, until the beginning of your next turn, whenever you or a creature within 5 feet of you is hit by a ranged weapon attack, including the attack that triggered this ability, you can reduce the damage taken from the attack by 1d10 + your Dexterity modifier + your monk level.

Step of the Wind

Beginning at 9th level, your training and skill enable you to move easily through a battlefield. You can take a bonus action on each of your turns in combat. This action can be used only to take the Dash, Disengage, or Dodge action.

Beginning at 13th level, you can spend 1 energy point to take two of these actions as a bonus action instead.

Focused Aim

At 10th level, when you miss with an attack roll, you can spend 1 to 3 energy points to increase your attack roll by 2 for each of these energy points you spend, potentially turning the miss into a hit.

Universal Mind

At 14th level, you understand all spoken languages. Moreover, any creature that can understand a language can understand what you say.

Timeless Body

Beginning at 14th level, your energy sustains you so that you suffer none of the frailty of old age, and you can't be aged magically. In addition, you no longer need food, water, or sleep, you are immune to disease, and whenever you finish a short rest, your exhaustion level, if any, is decreased by 1.

Uncanny Metabolism

Beginning at 15th level, when you roll initiative, you can regain all expended energy points. When you do so, roll your Martial Arts die, and you regain a number of hit points equal to your monk level plus the number rolled.

Once you have used this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest.

Survivor

Beginning at 15th level, your mastery of self grants you proficiency in all saving throws, including death saving throws.

Eyes of Truth

At 15th level, you gain 10 feet of truesight, piercing through all disguises and deceptions.

Conquering Will

Beginning at 18th level, when you make a saving throw and fail, you can spend 1 energy point to reroll it and take the second result.

Diamond Soul

At 20th level, you have trained yourself beyond to go beyond the normal limits of mortal beings. When you use your Flurry of Blows ability, you can spend 1 additional energy point to take the Dodge action as part of the same bonus action.

In addition, as an action, you can spend 4 energy points to become invisible for 1 minute. During that time, you have resistance to all damage but force damage.
