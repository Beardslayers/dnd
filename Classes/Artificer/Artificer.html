Artificer

General Flavor

An inventor who uses their spells to create magical items.

Ability Summary

Half-Spellcaster [Utility, Buffs, Minor Healing]

Create Magic Items

Use Extra Magic Items

With unnatural speed, a gnome dashes away from the pursuing giant, and his armor opens to let him inside before sliding closed behind him. He turns around and clenches his fist, and the gem on his gauntlet begins to spark with lightning.

A slender elf slips behind a long tube of wrought iron, and as she pats it lovingly, it erupts with flames, burning the zombie horde.

As poison erupts from slits in the walls, an elderly woman cackles in amusement and strolls forward. A steel cage embedded in her chest pulses rhythmically, and she slams her steel prosthtetic arm through the stone door barring her way forward.

Masters of invention, artificers use ingenuity and magic to unlock extraordinary capabilities in objects. They see magic as a complex system just waiting to be decoded and harnessed by their spells and inventions.

Understanding and Application

Much of the world views magic as an unsolvable mystery, an enigma that can never be understood. To an artificer, on the other hand, magic is a puzzle, just waiting for the right pieces to be put into place. Their lives are dedicated to finding those pieces, and then to using their discovered knowledge to create great things. To an artificer, there is no such thing as the unknowable, only the as-yet-unknown.

While an artificer has some skill at improvising, their greatest ability is in preparation. While other classes create solutions in the moment, an artificer will create solutions weeks or months in advance, constructing the perfect device to solve a problem, no matter when that problem may come.

New Experiences

Not every craftsman is an artificer. Some people simply take their skills and apply them to creation. They may study to complete a specific job, but in general they are content with their knowledge and the skills they already have. An artificer is set apart by their insatiable appetite for new applications of their knowledge. They want to do new things, to change what can be done, and to create heaven-and-earth-defying tools. An artificer is proud of what they have done, but what they have done is in the past, and for an artificer, the greatest joy is in tomorrow.

Because artificers are not prone to improvisation, their adventuring capabilities may be weak as a solo wanderer. Without an adventuring party, artificers often find themselves becoming tied down, trapped in a town or city an unsure of where to go next. When paired with a party, however, an artificer will often be the most enthusiastic about visiting new places. An artificer may understand the importance of a job that requires staying in one location, and they will likely have a home base or two, where they keep their more fine-tuned or heavy-to-move tools. But they will always be looking for the next opportunity to leave, even if they come back home at the end.

Creating an Artificer

Since an artificer is defined by their curiosity and creativity, think about where those traits came from. Did a craftsman parent inspire them to always look and understand? Did a glimpse of a passing sorcerer spark their curiosity about the unknowns of the world? Or were they dismissed by academic snobs who were convinced they could never understand the truth?

Beyond those basic traits, consider what inspired your artificer to adventure. Curiosity and creativity could both be achieved in a quiet forge or workshop, staying in one place. So what made your character leave the comfort of a stationary life? Did they grow up in a small town with limited resources, and so had to leave in order to learn? Did they grow up near a university, but decide a practical education would be better? Or did they grow up on the move, and a life of adventure is actually fairly stationary compared to what they're used to?

You must have an Intelligence score of 13 or higher in order to multiclass into this class.
